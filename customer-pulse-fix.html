Customer Pulse: Why ZID Fixes It

Today's Customer Pulse reporting breaks because every step in the pipeline uses a different identifier. The fix is simple: use Zeta ID as the common spine from audience creation through reporting. No backwards translation. No fan-out. Same person, same demographics, same answer.

Today's Process
Five different ID types, one broken pipeline
Each system uses a different identifier and a different data source for demographics. The round-trip from email to cookie and back is not bijective — it fans out at both ends.

Why this breaks
The forward path (email → cookie) fans out 1:N. The reverse path (cookie → email) fans out N:M. The round-trip brings back people who were never targeted. Demographics come from a different table than the one used for targeting. Every campaign looks the same: bell curve peaking at 45–54, ~50/50 gender regardless of targeting.
Fixed Process
ZID as the common spine — same person at every step
Prospect audiences, demographics, audience export, impression logs, and Customer Pulse all key on the same Zeta ID. The backwards translation step is eliminated entirely.

Why this works
ZID is the common identifier at every step. The audience was built on ZIDs. The export grabbed cookies belonging to those ZIDs. The impression logs recorded which ZIDs were reached. Customer Pulse gets those same ZIDs. Demographics come from the same ZID-keyed table used during audience creation. No backwards translation. No fan-out. The person you targeted is the person you report on.

What this eliminates

Manual impression pulls — no DSP analytics team member manually pulling cookies and loading them as a matched list.
Reverse identity resolution — the cookie → email_md5 backwards translation that causes fan-out is gone entirely.
Table mismatch — targeting and reporting both use ZID-keyed demographics from the same source.
Phantom bell curves — demographics reflect the actual people who were targeted and reached.
